> exquisite corpse

Say what?

The Exquisite Corpse game was created in 1925 by Surrealists André Breton, Yves Tanguy, Jacques Prévert, and Marcel Duchamp to have fun making art in a collaborative, unusual way. They were inspired by the old parlor game, Consequences, where a sheet of paper is passed from person to person, each writing a bit more of the story before folding the paper so only the last line is visible to the next writer. This always yielded some quite absurd results, and the Surrealists were impassioned to transfer this process to the world of art. Traditionally, Exquisite Corpse is played where each artist draws a part of a body, folds the paper near the edge of their drawn segment, and passes the paper to the next artist for them to continue the drawing and create an unusual, original creature in the end.

For the modern era

Our task, inspired by this old Surrealist game, was to work with a partner, taking turns to create our own digital exquisite corpse. We first wrote out our own set of rules, and then built block upon block to develop a collaborative piece of art.

Our rules

  • Inspired by the pandemic.
  • Can be realistic and/or abstract.
  • Black, white, and gray.
  • Must have a pattern involved.
  • 2:1 ratio.
  • Composed with vector drawings.
  • We can both see the full piece during the process.
